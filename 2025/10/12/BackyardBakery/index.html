Backyard Bakery — Turn-Based Procedural Puzzle Game


Backyard Bakery is a turn-based procedural puzzle game designed to deliver a cozy, strategic experience where replayability, tranquility, and charm meet intelligent randomness. The player tends to a whimsical garden filled with playful critters and dynamic seasonal changes, managing resources while cultivating balance and growth.

Creative Vision

Backyard Bakery reimagines the puzzle genre through non-violent, relaxing gameplay focused on curiosity and adaptation. Each playthrough feels fresh due to random variations in critter behavior and resource placement. The tone is tranquil yet strategic, encouraging players to plan ahead and react to the unpredictable rhythm of nature.

Role, Responsibilities, Tools

Role: System Designer and Co-Producer
Responsibilities: Core system balancing, procedural generation tuning, critter AI iteration, and player movement refinement. Assisted with milestone documentation, playtesting feedback analysis, and pipeline coordination with Terra.
Tools: Unreal Engine, Trello, GitHub, Figma, Excel, and version-control logs for level data tracking.

Core Concepts

Procedural Puzzle Design
Every playthrough introduces unique outcomes through random elements in critter movement and seed distribution. This procedural core ensures constant variety and replay value.

Non-Violent Interaction
Players don’t attack or destroy — they interact gently. “Booping” critters encourages them to drop seeds, which can be used to grow new plants or resources for future turns.

Replayability and Strategy
The combination of randomness and player planning produces a subtle tension between order and chaos. Success comes from observation, prediction, and efficient use of limited turns.

Gameplay Experience


Resource Management
Players collect seeds, plant bushes, and manage a limited inventory to sustain growth. Seasonal cycles subtly alter resource availability, requiring adaptive strategies.

Dynamic Challenge
Animal AI evolves each level, forcing new approaches to seed gathering and spatial control.

Tranquility with Depth
Though serene in tone, Backyard Bakery maintains intellectual depth through pattern recognition and system mastery. The environment and art direction reinforce a cozy, welcoming atmosphere.

Systems Overview

Core Systems:

Procedural Resource Distribution — Randomized seed and bush locations, rebalanced for fairness.

Critter AI — Each critter follows playful, semi-predictive patterns with small RNG offsets.

Seasonal Framework — Spring growth, summer bloom, autumn scarcity, and winter dormancy each affect strategy.

Level Design — 9–12 handcrafted maps built on tile-based grids, with difficulty rising progressively.

Progression Curve:
Levels 1–4 ease the player into resource loops and basic critter behavior. Levels 5–6 introduce mid-game randomness and sharper challenge spikes. Levels 7–9 (and optional 10–12) push optimization and pattern recognition to the limit.

Production Overview

Team Size: Two primary developers — Terra and ZD.
Scope: Nine core levels with procedural puzzle logic, scalable to twelve for final release.
Current Status:

Core systems functional (RNG and critter AI).

Level prototypes complete.

Art, style, and optimization polishing underway.
Milestone Focus:
Refine AI behavior, complete level balancing, and polish UI components such as credit screen, lose screen, and level select.

Design Goals

Replayability: Ensure every session feels different yet fair.

Accessibility: Maintain low cognitive load with readable feedback and simple controls.

Cohesion: Blend procedural systems and handcrafted design for meaningful progression.

Charm: Reinforce emotional tone through art, humor, and tranquil pacing.

Research Summary

Backyard Bakery applies the principle of meaningful play (Salen & Zimmerman, 2004) to build coherent cause-and-effect relationships within a procedural framework. Iterative playcentric testing (Fullerton, 2018) was used to balance RNG fairness and difficulty progression. Hunicke et al.’s (2004) MDA model guided the dynamic interplay between mechanical depth and aesthetic relaxation, while Deterding et al. (2011) informed reward structure and engagement pacing through gamified feedback systems.
